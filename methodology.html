How We Operate.

A brilliant mind in the wrong ecosystem is a liability. We don't just fill empty chairs; we engineer leadership structures that actually survive the fiscal year.

The LinkedIn
Paradox.

Let's be completely candid: LinkedIn is the most powerful professional network ever created. Everyone uses it. We use it. But here is the difference: Amateurs use it to spam keywords. We use it as a topographical map.

A profile tells us where an executive is sitting. It doesn't tell us if they are burning out, if they are carrying the weight of an incompetent board, or if their "SEO growth" was actually just an algorithm update they took credit for. We use the platform to find the signal, then we deploy human intelligence to decode the noise.

The 4-Phase Extraction

Read the files. Understand the logic.

01

The Reality Check

Before we touch a keyboard, we audit your expectations. If you want a VP of SEO who can also code React and manage paid media for $120k, we will respectfully decline the contract. Real care starts with absolute honesty about the market.

Case File: The Unicorn Hunt

A B2B SaaS client was struggling to fill a CMO role for 8 months. We audited their brief and realized their equity structure was severely misaligned with Series B standards. We forced a hard conversation with the board, restructured the offer, and filled the role with an industry titan in 14 days.

Signal Vs. Noise

This is where we map the network. We cross-reference public profiles with our private backchannels. We aren't looking for the person with the loudest voice on social media; we are looking for the operators who are quietly moving the needle.

The Interrogation

We don't do "tell me about your greatest weakness" interviews. We do cultural forensic analysis. How do they handle a sudden algorithm penalty? How do they manage a CFO who doesn't understand SEO? We stress-test the human being, not just the resume.

Case File: The Culture Clash

We sourced a brilliant candidate for a Series C startup. On paper, perfect. But during our behavioral deep-dive, we discovered his leadership style was highly autocratic. The startup's culture was hyper-collaborative. We killed the placement ourselves, protecting the client from a toxic cultural implosion, and brought in a collaborative leader instead.
